SIXT Share · Research Plan

Drop-Off Zones — Think-Aloud User Test

A moderated, think-aloud usability study to validate whether customers understand the new Drop-Off Zone concept, feel confident about end-of-rental pricing, and to choose between three visualisation concepts (Subtle, Adaptive, Prominent).

Method · Moderated think-aloud, 1:1 Duration · 45 min Participants · 6–8 Format · Interactive HTML prototype on phone

1 Background & rationale

SIXT Share customers regularly end rentals in low-demand areas, forcing employees to reposition vehicles to profitable locations. To nudge users toward better drop-off behaviour we are introducing Drop-Off Zones:

Reward zone · −€1 Standard zone · no change Paid zone · +€1 No-parking zone · cannot end Outside service area · cannot end
Important hypothesis to test. Only the end-of-rental zone affects the price. The starting zone is irrelevant. We therefore suspect that surfacing zones during the entire drive could confuse rather than help users. This study should produce evidence for or against that hypothesis.

2 Research goals

  1. Find out whether users understand the zone concept (types, meaning, where it applies).
  2. Measure their confidence in the final price after a rental ends in a paid or reward zone.
  3. Choose a preferred visualisation concept among Subtle, Adaptive, and Prominent.
  4. Validate colour assignment per zone type (green / yellow / red / grey).
  5. Decide if zones should be visible by default on the map and whether a toggle is needed.
  6. Sense-check the hypothesis that in-trip zone display has little value.

3 Research questions

RQ1
Comprehension. Can users correctly describe what each zone type means and what happens when they end the rental in it?
Probe: Standard, Paid, Reward, No-Parking, Outside Service Area.
RQ2
Cost confidence. After the end-of-rental screen, do users feel certain the fee/discount is correct, and can they explain how it was calculated?
RQ3
Concept preference. Which of Subtle, Adaptive or Prominent feels most helpful, least intrusive, and most trustworthy — and why?
RQ4
Colour mapping. Are the colours intuitive? Do they correctly suggest good, neutral, cost, and forbidden?
RQ5
Default visibility. Should the zone overlay be on by default, off by default, or contextual (only when relevant)?
RQ6
Toggle control. Do users want explicit control to show / hide zones on the map?
RQ7
Driving-screen relevance. Do users find zone information useful while driving, or only when ending the rental?

4 Method & setup

Format

Moderated, remote (Zoom) or in-person. Think-aloud protocol. Screen + face shared.

Stimulus

Interactive HTML prototype shown on the participant's own phone (mirrored for the moderator).

Counterbalancing

Order of the three concepts (Subtle / Adaptive / Prominent) rotated using a Latin square to control bias.

Recording

Screen, audio, and webcam (with consent). Observer takes structured notes.

Participants (n = 6–8)

  • Active SIXT Share customers (≥ 1 rental in last 3 months).
  • Mix of city centres with high zone density (e.g. Munich, Berlin, Hamburg).
  • Mix of frequent (≥ 4 trips / month) and occasional users.
  • Age and gender balanced; include at least 2 participants who have never noticed an in-app banner before (low-attention baseline).

Materials checklist

  • Prototype URL (current build, all three tweak states pre-configured).
  • Moderator script & observation grid.
  • Concept-card images for the post-task ranking exercise.
  • Colour-swatch sheet for the open colour-mapping exercise.
  • Consent form, incentive voucher.

6 Tasks

Task 0 — Cold first impression

≈ 4 min

Participant opens the driving screen mid-rental. The current zone is a Paid zone. No explanation has been given.

Ask: "Tell me everything you see on this screen. What do you think is happening? What would happen if you ended the rental right now?"
Probes: Did they notice the zone indicator? What did they assume the colour meant? Did they look for a price?

Task 1 — End rental in a Reward zone

≈ 4 min

"You've finished your errand. You'd like to end the rental here." Vehicle is currently inside a Reward zone.

Ask: "Walk me through how you would end the rental. Once you're at the summary, tell me how the final price came together."
Probes: Do they spot the −€1? Do they understand why they got it? Would they have parked here on purpose?

Task 2 — End rental in a Paid zone (with alternative offered)

≈ 5 min

Vehicle is in a Paid zone. A nearby free / reward zone is shown as an alternative.

Ask: "End the rental here, or somewhere else — your choice. Talk me through your decision."
Probes: Trust in the +€1 fee? Effort vs. reward of moving? Clarity of the alternative banner?

Task 3 — Attempting to park in a No-Parking zone

≈ 3 min

Vehicle is in a No-Parking zone inside the service area.

Ask: "Try to end the rental here."
Probes: Do they understand why they can't end it? Is the message distinct enough from "outside service area"?

7 Concept comparison block

Show each concept on the same Paid-zone scenario. Counterbalance order.

Subtle

Zones overlaid on map only; no banner on driving screen. Cost surfaces at end of rental.

Adaptive

Map overlay + contextual banner that appears only when in a paid / reward / no-park zone.

Prominent

Persistent zone callout above the bottom sheet, plus map overlay, plus alternative-zone CTA.

Questions for each concept

  • "What does this version tell you that the others don't?"
  • "How distracting / intrusive does it feel while driving?" (1–5)
  • "How confident are you about the final price?" (1–5)
  • "How likely would you be to change where you park because of this?" (1–5)

Close the block with a forced ranking: "Drag these three into the order you'd want the app to behave."

8 Colour mapping exercise

Show five blank zone shapes and a palette of 8 swatches (green, yellow, red, grey, blue, purple, orange, white). Ask participants to assign one colour to each zone type before seeing the current design. Then reveal the current mapping and discuss agreement / disagreement.

Why blind first? Mapping colours after seeing ours biases them. Capturing their unprimed choice tells us how natural our palette is.

9 Default visibility & toggle

  1. Show the map with zones visible. Ask: "Would you want to see this every time you open the app, only when relevant, or never unless you ask?"
  2. Show the map with zones hidden + a "Show zones" toggle. Ask: "Would you use this toggle? When?"
  3. Force a trade-off: "If you could only have one — zones-on-by-default or a toggle — which would you keep?"

10 Wrap-up

  • "On a scale of 0–10, how likely are you to recommend SIXT Share more if this feature shipped?" (delta question)
  • "What's the one thing you'd change?"
  • "If you could turn this feature off entirely, would you?"

12 What we will not test

  • Exact pricing amounts (€1 placeholder is sufficient).
  • Push-notification behaviour outside the app.
  • End-to-end booking flow before the rental starts.
  • The map overlay's visual fidelity at extreme zoom levels.

13 Risks & mitigations

RiskMitigation
Participants over-rationalise the concept once primed.Capture cold first impression (Task 0) before any explanation.
Order bias across the three concepts.Latin-square counterbalancing.
Halo effect — they say they "like" Prominent because it's loudest.Pair preference with confidence + intrusiveness ratings; force a ranking.
Lab vs. real driving context.Frame each task with a concrete real-life scenario; ask about last actual trip.
